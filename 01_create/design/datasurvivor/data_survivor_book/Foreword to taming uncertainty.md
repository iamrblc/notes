# Foreword

This course heavily relies on Statistical Rethinking (both the 2nd edition book and the 2023 lecture series available on YouTube) from Richard McElreath. Therefore, unless otherwise noted, all references, quotes, examples are from these resources.

However, while the aforementioned works serve as the backbone of this course, I will make substantial modifications:

- Richard is an amazing writer and I adore his cultural outlooks and references. However, while I enjoy reading about the evolution of scientific concepts, I find many of the examples being too distractive. So I sum those up briefly or cut them out.
- For the same reason, I will use a more "traditional" nomenclature 
- He gives excellent perspectives on certain ideas, but I prefer to elaborate some of these deeper for better understanding or for better suitability to our everyday research quests. 
- Instead of using his specially crafted `rethinking` R library (or Python packages developed by his wonderful disciples) I will use available and widely used statistical tools (eg. Python packages or R libraries, solutions in JASP, etc.).
- It's also likely that I will just skip a few parts if I don't find them useful _for our actual_ work or understanding. 

With all these, I can't emphasize how much I recommend either his book and/or his video lectures. Those are game changers even if you end up using only Fisherian statistics. [^1]
