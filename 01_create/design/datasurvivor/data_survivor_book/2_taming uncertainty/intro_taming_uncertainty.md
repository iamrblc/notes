# Taming Uncertainty - Deep dive into inferential statistics

In the first part of the _Data Survivor Handbook_, we explored data from many angles: we filled our toolbox with data-handling utensils, learned how to ask meaningful questions, harnessed the power of visualization to uncover hidden patterns, and even delved into the principles of building compelling narratives with — and from — data.

If you're still reading, there's a good chance that Part I served as a gateway drug — just as I intended — and now you're ready for an even deeper trip into data-land. If that was the high, now comes the depth.

Ask everyday people what they think _statistics_ is, and they’ll usually describe what we’ve covered so far: descriptive statistics. This is what most of us encounter in everyday life. Election exit polls, stock market summaries, the weather forecast section on a news portal — these are all manifestations of descriptive statistics. They describe what your _available_ data contains.

Yes, this is the kind of statistics Winston Churchill supposedly only trusted when he himself falsified it. It is also the one that proudly sits at the pinnacle of the hierarchy of deception that begins with lies, then damned lies — as allegedly crafted by another Brit, former prime minister Benjamin Disraeli.

But there’s another face of statistics: **inferential statistics**.

If descriptive statistics is a — sometimes broken, sometimes foggy — mirror, then inferential statistics is the crystal ball that shows you the bigger picture. If descriptive statistics is the enigmatic smile of Mona Lisa in the painting, inferential statistics is the model herself with all her bodily sensations and thoughts when she posed for da Vinci. 

Inferential statistics gives you the broader picture. It helps you understand the world beyond your data sample: to identify what treatments work, optimize production, draw causal links, and even forecast the future.

Ask scientists what they think statistics is, and they’ll most likely refer to inferential statistics.
