
**Citizen Science** –  
The Hungarian Ornithological and Nature Conservation Society collects stork nesting data from villagers across the Great Hungarian Plain, who report yearly nest occupancy via a mobile app.

---

**Participatory Research** –  
Researchers from a Budapest university work with residents of a panel housing estate to jointly design a study on summer heat stress, deciding together where temperature sensors should be placed and what daily experiences should be documented.

---

**Participatory Action Research (PAR)** –  
Parents, teachers, and researchers in a small-town primary school collaborate to investigate early school leaving, analyze the causes together, and implement and evaluate new mentoring practices.

---

**Community-Based Research** –  
A village near Lake Balaton partners with environmental scientists to study increasing algae blooms, with research questions shaped through local forums involving fishermen, tourism operators, and residents.

---

**Co-Creation** –  
Roma youth, NGO workers, and social researchers co-develop and test a local employment training program, refining the approach based on participant feedback and lived experience.

---

**Crowdsourcing** –  
Volunteers across Hungary transcribe handwritten census records from the Austro-Hungarian Monarchy period to make them digitally searchable for historians and genealogists.

---

**Transdisciplinary Research** –  
Urban planners, sociologists, transport engineers, disability advocates, and municipal officials in Budapest jointly design and evaluate accessibility improvements in tram and metro systems.

Mertonian norms (Robert Merton)
- communism/**communality:** all scientists should have common ownership of scientific goods (intellectual property), to promote collective collaboration; secrecy is the opposite of this norm.
- **universalism**: scientific validity is independent of the sociopolitical status/personal attributes of its participants.
- **disinterestedness:** scientific institutions act for the benefit of a comComon scientific enterprise, rather than for specific outcomes or the resulting personal gain of individuals within them.
- **organized skepticism:** scientific claims should be exposed to critical scrutiny before being accepted: both in methodology and institutional codes of conduct.

## Alan Irwin

**Citizen science as democratizing science**

- Rooted in STS (1995 book _Citizen Science_)
    
- Focus on **who defines problems**
    
- Citizens as **knowledge actors**
    
- Critique of deficit model
    
- Concerned with power, governance, legitimacy
    

👉 Key question:

## Rick Bonney

**Citizen science as public participation in research**

- Rooted in ecology practice (mid-1990s, Cornell Lab)
    
- Focus on **data collection & large-scale monitoring**
    
- Scientists design projects
    
- Citizens contribute observations
    
- Emphasis on scientific productivity & education
    

👉 Key question:
