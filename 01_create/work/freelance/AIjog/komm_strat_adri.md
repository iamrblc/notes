## Communication Strategy — Key Principles to Emphasize

### 1. **Audience-Centered Approach**

- **Primary audience:** international professionals (AI & law scholars, policy experts, judges, institutional stakeholders).
    
- **Secondary audience (later phase):** general Hungarian public — approached carefully and non-politically, focusing on education and awareness.
    
- Principle: **tone and content adapted to audience maturity**, but core messages consistent across all levels.
    

---

### 2. **Clarity and Credibility**

- All communication should reinforce the project’s **scientific credibility** and **independence**.
    
- Emphasis on _evidence-based outputs_, transparent sourcing, and accessibility (especially for policymakers and courts).
    
- Key value: **authoritative but approachable** communication.
    

---

### 3. **Integration with Dissemination Activities**

- The communication plan shouldn’t be separate — it should **amplify** the dissemination pillars already listed (website, blogs, conferences, publications, newsletters).
    
- Events, working papers, and the open-access database should be **communication assets** — each producing shareable stories, visuals, and updates.
    

---

### 4. **Consistency and Branding**

- Visual and narrative consistency across the website, social media, and newsletters.
    
- Develop a **coherent project identity**: clear name, tagline, tone, and visual style reflecting trust, expertise, and transparency.
    

---

### 5. **Phased Expansion**

- **Phase 1 (Years 1–2):** English-language, expert-oriented communication (visibility in global academic & policy networks).
    
- **Phase 2 (Years 3+):** selective Hungarian outreach — focus on explaining impacts, not advocacy.
    
- Principle: **grow visibility strategically** without overexposure.
    

---

### 6. **Impact Orientation & KPIs**

- Define **realistic indicators** aligned with project goals:
    
    - Reach: website traffic, newsletter subscribers, social media engagement.
        
    - Recognition: invitations to speak, media appearances, policy consultations.
        
    - Conversion: citations, collaborations, references in policy documents.
        
- Focus on **quality of attention** rather than just quantity.
    

---

### 7. **Ethical and Context-Sensitive Communication**

- Always respect political neutrality and academic independence.
    
- Transparency in presenting results and acknowledging limitations.
    
- Awareness of Hungary’s current media and political environment — **avoid polarization**, highlight expertise and contribution to good governance.
    

---

### 8. **Synergy with Partners and Networks**

- Build visibility through **existing networks** (COST Actions, European research consortia, universities, AI ethics initiatives).
    
- Leverage **reciprocal communication** — guest posts, joint webinars, shared newsletters — to extend reach beyond project resources.
    

---

### 9. **Sustainability**

- Plan communication with an eye to **legacy**: ensuring materials (database, publications, website) remain accessible and useful after project completion.
    
- Position the project as a **long-term reference point** in the field.
    

---
